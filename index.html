Inside the film

A pouch is three decisions,
laminated together.

What keeps chips crisp for nine months isn't the print — it's the structure behind it. Scroll through the layers we bond for every job.

Print · BOPP / PET
Barrier · MET-PET / AL
Seal · LLDPE / CPP

One film. Your structure.

01

The print carrier

Your artwork is reverse-printed in up to eight colours on rotogravure — so the ink sits inside the laminate. It can't scuff, scratch or fade on shelf, no matter how rough the supply chain is.

02

The barrier core

Metallised PET or aluminium foil blocks oxygen, moisture and light. This layer is why roasted, fried and dried products stay fresh for months — and it's specified per product, not per catalogue.

03

The food-contact seal

LLDPE or CPP on the inside — food-grade, heat-sealable, and strong enough to run on high-speed filling lines without splitting a single seam.

04

Bonded into one film

Solvent-less lamination fuses the layers into a single web — then it's slit, pouched and batch-tested in our own QC lab before dispatch. Common structures we run:

BOPP / MET-PET / PE PET / AL / LLDPE Mono LLDPE — recyclable CPP / AL / PET PET / PE
