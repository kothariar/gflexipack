## The strategy in one screen

**Why clusters, not random posts.** Google and AI both reward *topical authority* — a hub of interlinked pages that together prove you own a subject. We build 6 clusters. Each has one **pillar** (broad, evergreen, the internal-link hub) and several **spokes** (specific, long-tail) that link up to the pillar. This is what makes Sprinpak rank and what gets us cited by AI.

**Six clusters (mapped to the topic bank):**

| # | Cluster | Why it ranks / gets cited | Ties to money pages |
|---|---|---|---|
| A | **Buyer's Guide & Decision** | Decision-stage, highest intent, most AI-quoted ("how to choose…") | Inquiry form |
| B | **Materials & Technical** | Educational authority; the definitions AI extracts verbatim | Product pages |
| C | **Product Formats** | One high-intent keyword per format | 13 product pages |
| D | **Industry Applications** | Long-tail, hardest-converting, mirrors competitor "Markets" pages | 7 industry pages |
| E | **Sustainability & Compliance** | Uncontested + rising-search; strong GEO surface | EPR/recyclable angle |
| F | **Trust, Quality, Ops & Export** | Credibility signals buyers + AI weigh (ISO, ASTM, Mundra) | About / capabilities |

**Front-loading logic:** Months 1–2 ship the pillars + the FAQ/"how-to-choose" magnets that AI cites fastest and that support every later post. Months 3–4 go wide on formats + industries (the conversion long-tail). Months 5–6 deep-dive differentiators (low MOQ, Gujarat, export, EPR, ASTM lab) and refresh/expand winners.

**Legend:** `[PILLAR]` hub page · `[GEO]` AI-citation magnet (FAQ/definition/checklist) · `[BUY]` high buyer intent · `[EDU]` top-of-funnel · `[TRUST]` credibility. Each post lists its **primary keyword**.

## Publishing mechanics that make this rank (do these, not just the writing)

**1. Internal linking (the multiplier).**
- Every spoke links up to its pillar; every pillar links down to its spokes.
- Every format post (C) links to relevant industry posts (D) and vice-versa (e.g. Spout pouch ⇄ ORS/juice).
- Every buyer-guide post (A) ends with a link to the **inquiry form** (`/#inquiry`).

**2. Schema markup per post type.**
- FAQ-style posts → `FAQPage` JSON-LD (this is what wins AI Overviews + rich results).
- All posts → `Article` / `BlogPosting` with author, datePublished, dateModified.
- Product/industry posts → link to the matching `Product` schema on the landing page.

**3. Format for AI extraction (GEO).**
- Lead every `[GEO]` post with a **direct 40–60 word answer** to the title question, then expand. AI engines quote the concise answer.
- Use real numbers (MOQ 5,000; 300 MT/mo; 3–5 week lead; ISO 9001:2015; Mundra). Specifics get cited; vague claims don't.
- Add a short **FAQ block** (3–5 Q&As) at the foot of every post.

**4. On-page SEO basics per post.**
- One primary keyword in the H1, URL slug, first 100 words, and one H2.
- Descriptive alt text on every product image (you have real photos — use them).
- 800–1,500 words for pillars/guides; 500–800 for format/industry/FAQ posts.

**5. Distribution (content that isn't seen doesn't rank).**
- Repurpose each post into a LinkedIn post + a WhatsApp-shareable card (your B2B India channels).
- Feed key facts into your **IndiaMART / TradeIndia** listings — directories are ~42% of AI citations.
- Submit new URLs in Google Search Console; keep `sitemap.xml` current.

**6. Measure + double down.**
- Monthly: check Search Console for impressions/clicks per post; note which get AI-Overview mentions.
- The Month 6 refresh sprint targets whichever 5 posts show the most impressions-but-low-clicks — expanding those is the cheapest ranking gain available.
